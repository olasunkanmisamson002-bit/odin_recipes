Minestrone

Minestrone is a hearty Italian soup packed with vegetables, beans, and pasta or rice, simmered together in a rich tomato or vegetable broth. Its name comes from the Italian word "minestra," meaning soup, and there's no single fixed recipe — minestrone varies from region to region and even household to household, often depending on whatever fresh vegetables are in season.

What makes minestrone special is its versatility and comforting texture — a satisfying mix of soft vegetables, tender beans, and a small bite of pasta in every spoonful. It's typically served warm, topped with a sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese and a drizzle of olive oil, making it a filling, wholesome meal on its own, especially popular during the cooler months.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 can (15 oz) kidney beans, drained
  • 1 cup small pasta (like ditalini)
  • 2 cups spinach or kale, chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese, for serving

Steps

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat.
  2. Add the onion, carrots, and celery, and cook for 5 minutes until softened.
  3. Stir in the garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  4. Add the zucchini and diced tomatoes, and stir to combine.
  5. Pour in the vegetable broth and bring the mixture to a boil.
  6. Reduce the heat and let it simmer for 15 minutes.
  7. Add the kidney beans and pasta, and cook for another 10 minutes, or until the pasta is tender.
  8. Stir in the spinach or kale and cook for 2 more minutes until wilted.
  9. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  10. Serve warm, topped with grated Parmesan cheese.
